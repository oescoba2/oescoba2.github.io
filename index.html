About Me

"Aim for the moon. If you miss, you may hit a star, " Clement Stone once stated. As a young boy, I took that to heart, watching the NASA channel and yearning for the exploration of the vastness of our atmosphere and galaxy around us, and became passionate about rockets, computational fluid dynamics, and Machine Learning. All encompassed in Applied and Computational Mathematics.

I have found a true calling in taking complex mathematically involved engineering problems and turning them into bite-size pieces and then bridging the gaps of what is yet unknown with a precise solution. My current project deals with calculating nozzle and propulsion configurations for a hybrid rocket using a self-created Python script of Computational Fluid Dynamics. The aim is to able to provide the best thrust and impulse for our Utah-NASA funded rocket.

I also very much enjoy working in improving a production Machine Learning model that extracts genealogical information from a digital input that will allow users to find family information much easier than sorting through records.

Education

I am currently pursuing a Bachelor's of Science in Applied and Computational Mathematics Emphasis (ACME) from Brigham Young University. My anticipated graduation date is in April 2025. As part of the program, I am obtaining an emphasis (a minor) in Aerospace Engineering and Machine Learning. I wanted my emphasis to reflect my two biggest interests and give me foundational and technical projects and experience to be better suited for highly technical roles.

Aerospace

Currently I am doing coursework in Thermodynamics aimed at improving my first and second law analysis as well as improving my thermodynamic system analysis. I will be taking a course in Fluid Mechanics next semester too. I also plan on pursuing further coursework in heat transfer, computational fluid dynamics, finite element analysis, and complete the hybrid rocket engine capstone project with the Ira Fulton College of Mechanical Engineering my senior year.

Mathematics and Machine Learning

For Machine Learning, I am currently taking a course on algorithms and approximations. I am currently learning about various Data Structures, complexity analysis, and probability and sampling. Concurrently to this course, I am also doing a lab class that is focused on creating in Python3 the data structures we are learning as well learn numerical optimization and other important numerical linear algebra algorithms with focus on to images, networks, and data science. In my senior year, I will pursue courses focused on modeling, machine learning theory, and system controls.
