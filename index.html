Current Projects and Activities

BYU Rocketry Club: Hybrid Rocket Research Team

I joined the BYU Rocketry Club: Hybrid Rocket Research Team back in the fall of 2021. My friend Brennen invited me to come what the club was about and the rest is history. I have always had a big affinity to the cosmos and intricacies of astronautics and the engineering and mathematics contained therein. At the time I joined, the club, overseen by Dr. Gorrell, was the recipient of a grant from the Utah-NASA Consortium of about $8,000 that was to be used to generate research for paraffin LOX hybrid fuel. We created a design for a 12ft rocket that would reach a target of 10,000 ft and have a maiden voyage in the summer of 2022. We were able to present and publish our findings with the Utah-NASA consortium webpage in the spring of 2022 (image of team given above)

I was working within the propulsion subteam to create the LOX dome, combustion chamber, fuel grain, and nozzle. I was tasked with implementing a design for the nozzle configurations that would enable us to reach a target exit mach number of 3.1. I studied the works of Gas Dynamics by Zucrow and learned more about the method characteristics employed for nozzle design. I was able to implement a Python3 script that would similate axisymmetric irrotational compressible given a throat area, radius of curvarture, desired exit mach number, and specific heat of the fluid. The script would output two solutions: 1. Rao nozzle in 3D (using Matplotlib) and a txt or csv or excel file of the nozzle characteristic points that would then be used to create a CAD model in solid works. I am now currently working in implementin numerical methods for the transonic region and in recreating the mesh using a more efficient method as well that incorporates flow that does not become sonic at the throat and then use chemical chain reactions for when the mixture is not ideal.

Currently, our goal is to design a small scaled motor for the rocket since we had to resize the piping and increase the design due to new parachutes that had to be purchased since the originals never came in and pipes that were unable to be bent correctly given our limited tools.
